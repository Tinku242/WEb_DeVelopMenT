Employee



          An ERP system stores all the data related to various business processes in a centralized database. This allows employees to access and update the data from anywhere at any time.ERP systems enable employees to collaborate with colleagues across different departments and locations.ERP systems provide real-time data on various business processes, such as production, inventory, and sales. This allows employees to monitor their performance and identify areas where they can improve.ERP systems automate many routine tasks, such as data entry, report generation, and inventory management. This frees up employees' time, allowing them to focus on more critical tasks that require their expertise.


















Manager



         An ERP system provides real-time data on various business processes, enabling managers to monitor the performance of their teams and the organization as a whole. Managers can track key performance indicators (KPIs), such as sales revenue, production output, and inventory levels, and identify areas where they need to improve. Managers can use an ERP system's reporting and analytics tools to generate custom reports and analyze data trends, helping them to identify opportunities for growth and optimize their operations. Managers can use an ERP system's messaging and chat features to communicate with their teams, share documents and files, and monitor progress on projects.An ERP system provides managers with a complete view of their organization's resources, including personnel, equipment, and inventory.











Admin



         An admin is responsible for managing the users of an ERP system and their access to various modules and functions. The admin can create and delete user accounts, assign roles and responsibilities, and control access to sensitive data.An admin can configure the system settings of an ERP system according to the organization's requirements. The admin can customize the user interface, configure email notifications, and set up workflows and approvals.An admin can manage the master data of an ERP system, such as product data, customer data, and vendor data. The admin can create, edit, and delete master data and ensure its accuracy and completeness.
